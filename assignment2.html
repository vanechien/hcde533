Assignment 2: Parametric Modelling with Grasshopper

By Vanessa Chien Lai - HCDE 533

Quick preview of my final Grasshopper construction kit!

STEP 1: DESIGN!
For this assignment, I wanted to use the parametric modeling in Grasshopper to come up with different polygons with a variation of cuts.
I was really inspired by some of the examples we saw in class, and decided to try cuts of different angles to see what kind of 3D shape I could come up with.
Ultimately, I came up with four polygons, each with rounded corners (radius of 5). I used the polygon component and used parametric inputs to determine the number of sides.
Other inputs I had were Y height of the rectangle (12), width (described below), as well as inputs for the move function, which was set to (width/-2) for the X movement and (radius-height) for the Y movement.

STEP 2: SET CARDBOARD WIDTH & ARRAYS.
For my cardboard cuts, I used an array component to vary the number of cuts I had on each polygon.
For my cardboard, I decided to make the cuts a bit thinner than the width of the cardboard (based on my experience with assignment 1).
I made the width of the cuts 4.8.

STEP: PRINT!
It was my first time using the laser cutter at the 8, so the settings were new to me and looked a little different form the ones at the MILL.
My first attempt at cutting resulted in a small fire (oops) which I was able to quickly put out.

My final pieces came together without a hitch!
I was able to make a lot of different structures with the 4 shapes I cut.

Laser cutter settings:
5% speed
