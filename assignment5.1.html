------------------------ MOLDING & CASTING PART 1 -------------------------------------------


STEP 1: SKETCHING & PLANNING
------
For this assignment, I wanted to do a simple shape so that I could focus on learning the proper mold design techniques.
I decided to make a little round ring dish, with a dip in the middle (kind of looks like a red blood cell?)
Picturing how the mold-for-a-mold would fit together was making my brain hurt, so I made a sketch.
I purposely drew one of the mold halves upside down, so that I could picture how the final silicone pieces would fit together.
I decided to make 4 registration marks as well as a funnel to pour the HydroStone in later.


STEP 2: MODELING & RENDERING
------
I began with a super simple curve, and used the Revolve function to revolve it around the Y axis.
This created my master part. It looked pretty good!


Next, I added some registration marks, a funnel, and a plane to cut the shapes in two halves of the mold.
Using the BooleanSplit command, I split all the shapes and then moved them aside.



I then used BooleanDifference to make the "holes" for the registration marks, and finally, BooleanUnioin to merge everything together.
For the container, I made only the walls, and created this tapered edge to account for any printing discrepancies.
I figured that having this type of edge might help me demold my silicone more easily as well!

STEP 3: PRINTING & SILICONE CASTING
------
Printing took WAY longer than I anticipated: 5 hours for the bottom half, 6 for the top, and another 4 for the container.
I did some light sanding, and it was ready for silicone.

I used this rice trick in order to guesstimate how much silicone I needed in volume. I poured it into two cups, and determined how much I needed.
For this project, I decided to use Mold Star 30 because of it's higher tensile strength.
I read that this would be good for resin casting, which I plan to do for my final project.
I decided to layer some plastic wrap in between the container and the shape mold, just in case the silicone leaked through.
It ended up being a container - plastic wrap - shape mold - silicone sandwich!
Following the instructions, I measured 100:96 for the A:B ratio by weight.


Demolding was pretty easy the first time, but the second time I demolded too early and caused some damage to the edges of the silicone.
Fortunately, the essential parts of the mold remained intact and the molds fit well!


Next week, I plan to cast 4 of these ring dishes in hydrostone, and plan to use some dye to tint it in different colors.
