STEP 1: DEMO, LEARNING, TESTING
------
This week, we had a very involved demo during class which I really appreciated. I'm a hands-on learner, and Junchao's demo was really helpful!
We were able to see how the mill worked up close, and even milled (or attempted to mill) a square.
Within 5 seconds the FR1 layer came off, along with the sacrificial layer... that's how we learned that the WHOLE surface needs double sided tape.
After testing it out I was ready to try it on my own!

STEP 2: PLANNING & MODELING
------
Next, I used my caliper to measure the thickness of the FR1 board and got 1.4mm.
Because I didn't want my finger joints to stick out, I chose to make the slots only 1mm deep.
Junchao mentioned that we needed to leave some wiggle room for the finger joints, so I made the joints 6.7mm and the slots 7mm (this was just a guesstimate).
I also added dogbones of 1mm in diameter, and a cute little bear for the engraving.
For unknown reasons, any shapes that were mirrored in Rhino were showing up wrong on KiCAD so I had to tweak my file a few times.
As instructed, I set my box cutting lines as Edge_Cuts and the bear as User_Drawings.

STEP 3: TIME TO MILL!
------
I struggled for a long time to get my GRB to look right on Bantam. For some reason, the bear kept getting distorted, and the face ended up shrinking.
It turned into this little mole that was actually quite cute. I decided to keep it :)

Here were the settings I used on the Bantam software:
Material size - 100mm x 70mm x 1.4mm (FR1 thickness)
Z-offset - 1.4mm (sacrificial layer, including double sided tape)
I also added a 5mm x 5mm offset so that the design wouldn't cut so close to the edge.

Attempt #1 cutting went smooth, but the pieces just would not fit together (and I did not want to sand them).
I went back to my Rhino file and trimmed the finger joints a bit more, making them 6.5mm for the 7mm slot.
I was able to fit the pieces together on Attempt #2! However, I learned that this material is very rigid and unforgiving.
My finger joint box was a little loose, which means I probably should have gone with 6.6mm. Oh well, good experience for next time.
