STEP 1: ASSEMBLE
------
Assembly for this printer took about 2 hours. I found the instructions a bit confusing, but manageable
(thank you IKEA for the experience with confusing furniture assembly instructions!)
Overall, I didn't have too much trouble, but I did have to unscrew pieces a couple times because I kept getting the cables on the wrong sides.
After connecting all the cables, I was ready to level the bed!

STEP 2: LEVELING THE BED.
------
Next, I did the leveling procedure for the bed! I had quite a bit of trouble at first because the back left corner was way too close to the nozzle.
After doing some online searching, I read that this is a pretty common issue! So I raised the z axis stopper slightly, giving me more wiggle room.
I printed the squares to check how well it was leveled (not pictured) and after some tweaks, I made another bed level test I found online.

STEP 3: TIME TO PRINT GEOMETRIC SHAPES!
------
Here's a quick overview of my STL models:

The cube prints were pretty straightforward. I printed low, standard, and super qualities, as well as the concentric setting.
Next, I printed the tubes, with single extrusion (0.4mm) and double extusion (0.8mm) walls.
Feeling a little more confident, I attempted to print 3 of the cylinders in one print. Even though they printed okay, I noticed a wonky waving on the side of the cylinders.
I also added small extruded labels to indicate 0.1mm and 0.001mm tolerance.

Here are all my geometric prints in one picture! I also recorded the sizes with my caliper (see chart below). Overall, the measurements were surprisingly accurate!

STEP 4: PRINTING NESTED SHAPES
------

Organic Vase Rhino Tutorial

After some struggle I realized it's because I needed some thickness in my extrusion, and extruding only lines always resulted in an error.
