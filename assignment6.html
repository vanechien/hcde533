Here's a quick recap of the things I did for part 1 of the assignment:


STEP 1: SKETCHING & PLANNING
------
For this assignment, I wanted to do a simple shape so that I could focus on learning the proper mold design techniques.
I decided to make a little round ring dish, with a dip in the middle (kind of looks like a red blood cell?)


STEP 2: MODELING & RENDERING
------
I began with a super simple curve, and used the Revolve function to revolve it around the Y axis.
This created my master part. It looked pretty good!


Next, I added some registration marks, a funnel, and a plane to cut the shapes in two halves of the mold.
Using the BooleanSplit command, I split all the shapes and then moved them aside.



I then used BooleanDifference to make the "holes" for the registration marks, and finally, BooleanUnioin to merge everything together.
For the container, I made only the walls, and created this tapered edge to account for any printing discrepancies.
I figured that having this type of edge might help me demold my silicone more easily as well!

STEP 3: PRINTING & SILICONE CASTING
------
Printing took WAY longer than I anticipated: 5 hours for the bottom half, 6 for the top, and another 4 for the container.
I did some light sanding, and it was ready for silicone.

------PART 2 OF THE ASSIGNMENT--------
For the hydrostone casting portion, I wanted to try recreating a terrazzo effect. Because I was unfamiliar with the propoerties of hydrostone (and my own mold), I tried casting a regular piece first.
Because my mold was thin and wide, I tried using some pens and rubber bands to keep the mold sandwiched together. It worked pretty well!

For the terrazzo dish, I first tinted some hydrostone using different acrylic paints, and spread each in a thin layer on aluminum foil.
After curing, I crushed the colors into little terrazzo "chips" and mixed them in a container.
Lastly, I mixed up hydrostone powder with water, and gradually added the terrazzo chips and poured it into the container.
My first terrazzo piece came out well, but I ran our of hydrostone at the end, which ended up making me add too much water.
